About This Text

ग्रंथपरिचय — An Introduction to Panchikaran

Panchikaran (पंचीकरण) is a foundational Advaita Vedānta treatise composed by Adi Shankaracharya (c. 788–820 CE). It explains the cosmic process by which the five subtle elements (Tanmatras) combine into the five gross elements (Sthula Mahābhūtas) of which all physical matter is made — and how understanding this process reveals the identity of the individual Self with Brahman.

हा ग्रंथ पंचीकरण प्रक्रिया सांगतो — ज्या प्रक्रियेने पाच सूक्ष्म तत्त्वांचे (तन्मात्रे) पाच स्थूल महाभूते बनतात. या प्रक्रियेचे ज्ञान जीव-ब्रह्म ऐक्य कसे दाखवते ते स्पष्ट करतो. श्रीमत्परमहंसपरिव्राजकाचार्य श्रीमद्शंकराचार्यांनी हा ग्रंथ रचला आहे.

This edition — Sartha Satik Panchikaran, Marathi — is a Marathi translation and detailed commentary in the tradition of Brahmanishtha Shriramguru, published by Tukaram Book Depot, Mumbai (3rd edition, 2008). It makes the Sanskrit text accessible through thorough Marathi explanation, illustrative analogies (Dṛṣṭānta), and systematic reference tables (Koshtaka).

Frequently Asked Questions

About the text, the teaching, and this website

What is Panchikaran? ▼
Panchikaran (पंचीकरण) is a short foundational text of Advaita Vedānta, composed by Adi Shankaracharya. It describes the cosmic process (Panchikaran — "fivefold combination") by which the five subtle elements become the five gross elements of the physical universe. More importantly, it uses this cosmological description to lead the student to the recognition that the individual Self (Jiva) is identical with Brahman — the universal, non-dual Pure Consciousness.
What does "Tat Tvam Asi" mean? ▼
"Tat Tvam Asi" (तत्त्वमसि) is a Mahavakya — a great Vedic declaration — from the Chandogya Upanishad. It means "That Thou Art." Tat (That) refers to Brahman — infinite, non-dual Pure Consciousness. Tvam (Thou) refers to your own deepest Self — not the body or mind, but the pure witness-Consciousness within. Asi (Art) declares their absolute identity. They are not two similar things — they are the same single Reality.
Who is Brahmanishtha Shriramguru? ▼
Brahmanishtha Shriramguru (ब्रह्मनिष्ठ श्रीरामगुरु) was a realized Advaita Vedānta teacher in whose tradition this Marathi commentary was composed. This edition — Sartha Satik Panchikaran — follows the lineage: Shankaracharya → Sridakshinananda Sarasvati → Sadeshika-shishya Jayakrishnaji. The text was published by Tukaram Book Depot, Mumbai (3rd edition, August 2008). The commentary was originally composed in Gujarati and later translated into Marathi.
What are the qualifications required to study this text? ▼
The text specifies four qualifications (Sadhana Chatushtaya): Viveka — the ability to discriminate between the real (Self/Atman) and the unreal (body/mind); Vairagya — genuine dispassion toward worldly pleasures; Shat-Sampatti — the sixfold inner wealth (Shama, Dama, Uparama, Titiksha, Shraddha, Samadhana); and Mumukshatva — the burning, intense desire for liberation. Even a partial version of these qualifications is sufficient to begin study — they deepen through practice.
