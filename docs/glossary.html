संस्कृत शब्दकोश

Sanskrit Glossary

28 key terms — Sanskrit · Marathi · English · with definitions in both languages

आत्मन्·आत्मा·Atman
Core Concepts

प्रत्येक प्राण्याचे खरे स्वरूप — शुद्ध चेतना. ब्रह्माशी अभिन्न. देह, मन, बुद्धी यांचा साक्षी. अजन्मा, नित्य, शाश्वत.

The innermost Self — Pure Consciousness that is the true nature of every being. Identical with Brahman. The witness (Sakshi) of body, mind, and intellect. Unborn, eternal, unchanging.

ब्रह्मन्·ब्रह्म·Brahman
Core Concepts

परम सत्य — अनंत, स्वयंप्रकाश, अद्वैत शुद्ध चेतना. एकमेव, द्वितीय नाही. सत्-चित्-आनंदरूप. सर्व सृष्टीचे मूळ कारण.

The Absolute Reality — infinite, self-luminous, non-dual Pure Consciousness. The one without a second. Sat-Chit-Ananda. The ultimate cause of all creation. What remains when all appearances are seen through.

पंचीकरण·पंचीकरण·Panchikaran
Core Concepts

प्रत्येक सूक्ष्म भूताचे दोन भाग होतात — अर्धे स्वत:चे राहते, उरलेल्याचे चार भाग होऊन इतर चार भूतांना दिले जातात. यामुळे पाच स्थूल महाभूते तयार होतात.

The fivefold combination: each subtle element gives half of itself pure, and distributes the other half as ⅛ each to the other four elements, producing the five gross elements from which all physical matter is made.

माया·माया·Maya
Core Concepts

ब्रह्माला नाम-रूप देणारी शक्ती. ती सत् नाही, असत् नाही — अनिर्वचनीय आहे. जगाची प्रतीती देणारी पण स्वतः भासमान शक्ती.

The cosmic power that makes Brahman appear as the world of multiplicity — names and forms. Neither real (it has no independent existence) nor unreal (it produces real-seeming effects) — it is inexplicable (Anirvachaniya). It is beginningless ignorance at the cosmic level.

अविद्या·अज्ञान·Avidya
Core Concepts

व्यक्तिगत अज्ञान — आत्म्यावर अनात्म्याचा अध्यास. सर्व दुःखाचे मूळ कारण. ज्ञानाने नाश पावते.

Individual ignorance — the superimposition of the non-Self (body, mind, ego) upon the Self. The root cause of all suffering, bondage, and rebirth. Destroyed instantly by the dawn of Self-knowledge, just as darkness is destroyed by light.

जीव·जीव·Jiva
Core Concepts

अंत:करणात प्रतिबिंबित झालेला आत्मा. उपाधींमुळे बद्ध वाटतो, परंतु खऱ्या अर्थाने मुक्तच आहे. जीव आणि ब्रह्म तत्त्वतः एकच.

The individual soul — Brahman reflected in the Antahkarana, appearing bound due to identification with the body-mind complex. The Jiva is essentially identical with Brahman, but appears limited due to Avidya and Upadhi. Recognizing this identity is Moksha.

उपाधि·उपाधी·Upadhi
Core Concepts

देह, मन, अहंकार — जे अनंत आत्म्याला मर्यादित वाटायला लावतात. रंगीत काचेसारखे — काच रंगलेली आहे, प्रकाश नाही. तशा उपाधी आहेत, आत्मा नाही.

Limiting adjuncts — the body, mind, and ego that appear to limit the infinite Self, like a coloured glass that appears to tint colourless light. The glass is coloured; the light is not. Remove the glass and the light is seen in its natural purity. Remove the Upadhi through Self-knowledge and the Self is seen in its natural infinity.

सत्-चित्-आनन्द·सत्-चित्-आनंद·Sat-Chit-Ananda
Core Concepts

अस्तित्व-ज्ञान-आनंद — ब्रह्माचे त्रिविध वर्णन. या तीन वेगळ्या नाहीत — एकच अखंड सत्य. सत् म्हणजे नाश नाही, चित् म्हणजे अज्ञान नाही, आनंद म्हणजे कमतरता नाही.

Existence-Consciousness-Bliss — the threefold description of Brahman's nature. Not three separate qualities but one indivisible Reality seen from three angles. Sat: Pure Existence that never ceases. Chit: Pure Consciousness that never dims. Ananda: Pure Bliss without cause or object.

त्रिपुटी·त्रिपुटी·Triputi
Core Concepts

जाणणारा, जाणले जाणारे आणि जाणण्याची क्रिया — ही त्रिपुटी निर्विकल्प समाधीत विलीन होते. तोपर्यंत हीच बंधनाचे कारण आहे.

The triad of knower (Jnata), known (Jneya), and the act of knowing (Jnana) — or perceiver, perceived, and perception. The entire experience of Samsara is made of Triputi. In Nirvikalpa Samadhi, this Triputi completely dissolves into pure undivided Consciousness.

कर्म·कर्म·Karma
Core Concepts

अहंकाराने केलेले कर्म संस्कार निर्माण करते जे भावी जन्माला कारण होते. तीन प्रकार: संचित, क्रियमाण, प्रारब्ध. ज्ञानाने संचित आणि क्रियमाण नष्ट होतात.

Action and its residual impressions. Three types: Sanchita (accumulated past Karma), Kriyamana (Karma being created now), Prarabdha (Karma ripening in this life). Self-knowledge destroys Sanchita and Kriyamana instantly; Prarabdha must be exhausted through experience even by the Jnani.

विवेक·विवेक·Viveka
Sadhana

नित्य-अनित्य, आत्मा-अनात्मा यांच्यात भेद करण्याची क्षमता. पहिले साधन. विवेकाशिवाय वैराग्य येत नाही.

Discrimination — the capacity to distinguish between the Real (Nitya/eternal) and the unreal (Anitya/impermanent), between the Self and the non-Self. The first and foundational qualification of the Vedanta student.

वैराग्य·वैराग्य·Vairagya
Sadhana

या लोकातले व परलोकातले भोगांसंबंधाने इच्छा राहिल्या (इच्छेचा अभाव) — त्याचे नाव वैराग्य. दुसरे साधन.

Dispassion — the natural falling away of craving for enjoyments of this world and the next, born from the recognition (through Viveka) that all such enjoyments are impermanent. Not forced suppression of desire but genuine disinterest born of wisdom.

मुमुक्षुता·मुमुक्षा·Mumukshatva
Sadhana

मोक्षाची तीव्र इच्छा — सर्व इच्छांपेक्षा बलवान असलेली मुक्तीची आकांक्षा. चौथे साधन. हे नसेल तर वेदान्ताचा अभ्यास निष्फळ.

The burning, intense desire for liberation — stronger than any other desire. The fourth and culminating qualification. Without Mumukshatva, the other three qualifications remain merely intellectual achievements. With intense Mumukshatva, even partial other qualifications suffice for the teaching to bear fruit.

षट्संपत्ती·षट्संपत्ती·Shat-Sampatti
Sadhana

वेदान्त अभ्यासासाठी आवश्यक सहा आंतरिक संपत्ती: शम (मनाचा निग्रह), दम (इंद्रियांचा निग्रह), उपरती (विषयांपासून माघार), तितिक्षा (सहनशीलता), श्रद्धा (विश्वास), समाधान (एकाग्रता).

The sixfold inner wealth required for Vedanta study: 1. Shama (control of mind), 2. Dama (control of sense organs), 3. Uparama (withdrawal from sense enjoyment), 4. Titiksha (endurance of pairs of opposites), 5. Shraddha (faith in Guru and scripture), 6. Samadhana (one-pointed concentration).

गुरु·गुरु·Guru
Sadhana

आत्मसाक्षात्कार झालेला सद्गुरू जो योग्य शिष्याला आत्मज्ञान देऊ शकतो. गुरू हेच ब्रह्म आहे. गुरूशिवाय आत्मज्ञान नाही.

The Self-realized spiritual teacher who is competent to transmit Self-knowledge to a qualified student. The Guru is not merely a teacher of information — the Guru is identified with Brahman itself, and the Guru's presence directly radiates Self-knowledge to the receptive student.

श्रवण·श्रवण·Shravana
Sadhana

ज्ञानयोगाचे पहिले साधन — सद्गुरूकडून उपनिषदांचे सावधपणे, वारंवार श्रवण.

Hearing — the first of the three disciplines of Jnana Yoga: systematic, attentive, repeated listening to the Upanishadic teaching from a qualified Guru. Not casual hearing but deep, wholehearted receptivity.

मनन·मनन·Manana
Sadhana

दुसरे साधन — ऐकलेल्या शिकवणीवर वारंवार विचार करून शंका दूर करणे. बौद्धिक विश्वास दृढ करणे.

Reflection — the second discipline: repeatedly turning the teaching over in the intellect to remove all doubts until the teaching becomes firm, unshakeable conviction rather than merely intellectual possibility.

निदिध्यासन·निदिध्यासन·Nididhyasana
Sadhana

तिसरे साधन — सत्याचे दीर्घ ध्यान करणे जेणेकरून सर्व विरोधी वासना नाश पावतात.

Contemplation — the third discipline: sustained, deep meditation on the truth of Brahman until all contrary habitual thought-patterns (Vasanas) are completely dissolved and the recognition of the Self is total, constant, and effortless.

सत्संग·सत्संग·Satsanga
Sadhana

साधुसमागम — आत्मज्ञानी महात्म्याचा सहवास. आध्यात्मिक उन्नतीचा सर्वोत्तम उपाय. संसाररूप समुद्र पार करण्याची नौका.

Company of Truth — being in the presence of a Self-realized sage or devoted seekers. Described in Panchikaran as the most powerful accelerant of spiritual development — even one moment of genuine Satsanga can dissolve Vasanas that years of solo practice could not touch.

पंचमहाभूत·पंचमहाभूते·Pancha Mahabhuta
Elements

आकाश, वायू, तेज, आप, पृथ्वी — पंचीकरणानंतर निर्माण झालेले पाच स्थूल महाभूते. सर्व भौतिक सृष्टी यांच्यापासून बनलेली आहे.

The five great elements — Akasha (Space), Vayu (Air), Tejas (Fire), Ap (Water), Prithvi (Earth) — that constitute all gross matter after Panchikaran. Everything in the physical universe is made of these five in different combinations.

तन्मात्र·तन्मात्र·Tanmatra
Elements

शब्द, स्पर्श, रूप, रस, गंध — पाच तन्मात्रे. पंचीकरणापूर्वीची सूक्ष्म भूतांची शुद्ध अवस्था. ही अत्यंत सूक्ष्म असतात — इंद्रियांना दिसत नाहीत.

The five subtle essences — Shabda (Sound), Sparsha (Touch), Rupa (Form), Rasa (Taste), Gandha (Smell) — that are the seed-forms of the five gross elements before Panchikaran. Too subtle to be perceived by the senses.

अंत:करण·अंत:करण·Antahkarana
Subtle Body

मन, बुद्धी, चित्त, अहंकार — या चार अंत:करणांचा एकत्र व्यवहार म्हणजे 'मन'. अंत:करण हेच जीवाचे मुख्य बंधन आहे.

The fourfold inner instrument: Manas (doubting deliberating mind), Buddhi (determining intellect), Chitta (memory and contemplation), Ahamkara (ego-sense). These four together constitute what we ordinarily call "the mind." The Antahkarana is the primary seat of bondage.

वासना·वासना·Vasana
Subtle Body

चित्तात साठलेले भूतकालीन कर्मांचे सूक्ष्म संस्कार. भविष्यातील इच्छा व पुनर्जन्माचे कारण. निदिध्यासनाने नाश पावतात.

Subtle impressions left by past actions and experiences, stored in the Chitta. They drive future desires, actions, and rebirths. Destroyed by Nididhyasana (sustained contemplation) and by Satsanga.

कोश·कोश·Kosha (Sheath)
Three Bodies

आत्म्याला झाकणारे पाच कोश: अन्नमय, प्राणमय, मनोमय, विज्ञानमय, आनंदमय. आत्मा या सर्वांचा साक्षी आहे, कोशरूप नाही.

The five sheaths that appear to cover the Self — Annamaya (physical), Pranamaya (vital), Manomaya (mental), Vijnanamaya (intellectual), Anandamaya (bliss). The Atman is the witness of all five sheaths — it is not identical with any of them.

तत्त्वमसि·तत्त्वमसि·Tat Tvam Asi
Mahavakya

'तू तेच आहेस' — छांदोग्य उपनिषदातील महावाक्य. 'तत्' = ब्रह्म, 'त्वम्' = जीव, 'असि' = पूर्ण अभिन्नता.

"That Thou Art" — the Mahavakya from Chandogya Upanishad (Samaveda). Declares the absolute identity of the individual Jiva (Tvam) and Brahman (Tat). The apparent contradiction is resolved through Bhaga-tyaga-lakshana.

अहं ब्रह्मास्मि·अहं ब्रह्मास्मि·Aham Brahmasmi
Mahavakya

'मी ब्रह्म आहे' — बृहदारण्यक उपनिषदातील महावाक्य. ही केवळ वाक्यरचना नाही — हा साक्षात्काराचा उद्गार आहे.

"I am Brahman" — the Mahavakya from Brihadaranyaka Upanishad (Yajurveda). The direct first-person assertion of identity with Brahman. Not a statement to be repeated as a mantra but a recognition to be lived as direct experience.

मोक्ष·मोक्ष·Moksha
Liberation

जन्ममृत्यूच्या चक्रातून कायमची मुक्ती. ब्रह्माशी अभिन्नतेच्या साक्षात्काराने. मोक्ष भविष्यात मिळवायचा नाही — आत्मा नित्यमुक्त आहे.

Liberation — permanent cessation of the cycle of birth and death through direct recognition of one's identity with Brahman. Moksha is not a future event or achievement — the Self is always already free. Practice only removes the ignorance that obscures this ever-present freedom.

समाधि·समाधी·Samadhi
Liberation

सविकल्प समाधीत त्रिपुटी असते; निर्विकल्प समाधीत शुद्ध अखंड चेतना असते. निर्विकल्प समाधी म्हणजे ब्रह्मसाक्षात्कार.

Complete meditative absorption. Savikalpa Samadhi retains subtle distinctions (Triputi). Nirvikalpa Samadhi is pure undivided Consciousness with no subject-object distinction whatsoever — this is the direct experience of Brahman and the gateway to final liberation.
